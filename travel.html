Region 01

North America

USA · New York · Washington DC · Miami

First stop. New York is everything you expect and nothing like you imagined -- Times Square, the Empire State, a rally outside Trump Tower, Central Park, Brooklyn and the view back. Washington for the monuments and a cemetery that puts everything in scale. Miami for the beach, Wynwood, and the clothes.

Region 02

South America

Colombia · Peru · Bolivia · Brazil · and more

Mountains, rainforest, colour and a pace of life that makes you question why you were ever in a rush. The continent where the university microgrid project stopped being abstract -- communities without reliable power are not a case study when you have spent time in them.

Region 03

Southeast Asia

Thailand · Vietnam · Cambodia · Indonesia · Bali

The region that probably changes you most the first time. The density of life, the heat, the noise -- and the contrast between the tourist trail and everything just off it. You realise quickly how much of the world runs on very little, and how much that does not stop people.

Region 04

Japan

Tokyo · Kyoto · Osaka · and more

Sleek and ancient at the same time. Tokyo moves faster than anywhere else on earth and Kyoto feels like it is from a different century entirely. A place that feels like two countries depending on where you are standing -- and one that rewards paying attention.

Region 05

Australia

Sydney · Melbourne · East Coast · Byron Bay

Four months living and working here, including a multi-level Melbourne venue. Cool, sunny, and the place that taught me most about integrating quickly into a team where you know nobody. The east coast is beautiful in a way that takes you by surprise.

Region 06

Africa

Kenya · Tanzania · and more

Hot, vast, and humbling. The kind of place that makes you very aware of how little of the world you actually know. The scale of everything -- the landscape, the wildlife, the contrast between what people have and what they build with it -- stays with you.

Region 07

Europe

UK · France · Spain · Italy · and more

The familiar made unfamiliar. Easier to travel, harder to pay attention. But different cities have different pulses -- and noticing that, the way a place moves and what people care about, is what keeps travel interesting long after the novelty wears off.

More than a gap year

What it actually did

Two years travelling gave me a real appreciation for how differently people live. The varying standards of living, the cultural contrasts, and the challenges communities face that often go unseen from home. I was also struck by how little people need to be genuinely content, and how quickly expectations rise with lifestyle.

Seeing the struggles of many communities, alongside the real beauty of the world, left me with a drive to do work that has impact beyond a project deliverable, and pointed me towards meaningful engineering as the direction I want my career to go.
