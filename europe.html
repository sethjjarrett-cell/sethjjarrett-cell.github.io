Region 06

Modern metropolitan Europe

Paris, Bosnia, Prague, Hamburg, Amsterdam, Italy, Athens. The familiar made unfamiliar -- easier to travel, harder to pay attention.

ParisBosniaPragueHamburgAmsterdamItalyAthens
Paris Bosnia Prague Hamburg Amsterdam Italy Athens

Stop 01

Paris

One of those cities where the reputation gets in the way of actually seeing it. It rewards walking without a plan.

Paris streets
The Eiffel Tower

The arrondissements away from the main sites have a completely different texture and feel more honest about what the city actually is day to day.

The obvious things
Eiffel Tower, the Louvre, Notre Dame. Do them -- they earned their status.
The neighbourhoods
Le Marais, Montmartre, Canal Saint-Martin. The real texture of the city.

Stop 02

Bosnia & Herzegovina

One of the most unexpectedly affecting countries in Europe. Sarajevo wears its recent history in a way that most European cities do not.

Mostar bridge
Sarajevo

The mix of Ottoman architecture, Austro-Hungarian buildings, and bullet holes in the walls is unlike anywhere else on the continent. Mostar and its rebuilt bridge are worth the trip alone.

Sarajevo
The history is recent and visible. The siege, the war tunnel, the scars on the buildings.
Mostar
The old bridge, the old town, the Neretva River. Beautiful and complicated.

Stop 03

Prague

One of the most architecturally intact cities in central Europe -- it survived the 20th century largely without being rebuilt.

Old Town Prague
Charles Bridge

The old town is genuinely beautiful rather than just historically significant. The astronomical clock, the Gothic churches, the medieval street plan.

Old Town Square
The astronomical clock, Gothic churches. Go early before it fills up.
Charles Bridge
At dawn or dusk. In the middle of the day it is hard to move.
The Vltava
Walking along the river gives you a different scale for the city.

Stop 04

Hamburg

A port city with the energy that port cities tend to have -- outward-looking, commercially serious, comfortable with being a bit rough at the edges.

Elbphilharmonie
Hamburg harbour

The Elbphilharmonie is extraordinary. The harbour district gives the city its particular character. The Sunday fish market at dawn is worth the early alarm.

Elbphilharmonie
The concert hall on the harbour. The building itself is as impressive as anything performed inside it.
Speicherstadt
Old warehouse district, now galleries and design studios. Brick canals, good coffee.
The harbour
One of the biggest ports in Europe. The Sunday fish market at dawn is worth it.

Stop 05

Amsterdam

A city that works better by bike than anywhere else in Europe. The canal rings, the narrow townhouses, the light on the water in the afternoon.

Amsterdam canals
By bike

Amsterdam is a well-designed city in the most literal sense -- the structure of it was thought through carefully and it shows.

The canals
Walk or cycle. The Jordaan neighbourhood is the best version of what Amsterdam does.
Rijksmuseum
Rembrandt, Vermeer, the Dutch Golden Age. Worth half a day.
By bike
The only way to move around. Rent one immediately.

Stop 06

Italy

Almost unfair in terms of what it offers per square kilometre. The food, the architecture, the art, the landscape.

Rome
Italian food

Every city is different and every city has centuries of things worth paying attention to. It is also a country where slowing down makes more sense than trying to cover ground.

Rome
Two thousand years of history. The Forum, the Colosseum, the Vatican. Overwhelming in the best way.
The food
Every region different. Pasta in Bologna, pizza in Naples, gelato everywhere.

Stop 07

Athens

Athens for the Athens Classic Marathon -- a pending chapter. Running the original marathon route to the Panathenaic Stadium.

The Acropolis
Athens streets

The city itself, the Acropolis, the neighbourhood below it, the food market. More to come on this one.

Athens Classic Marathon
Running the original route from Marathon to Athens. November 2025.
The Acropolis
The Parthenon above the city. More interesting than it sounds.
Monastiraki & Plaka
The neighbourhood below the Acropolis. Food, markets, the old city.
