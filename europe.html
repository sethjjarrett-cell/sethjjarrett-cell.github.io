Modern,
metropolitan Europe

Paris, Bosnia, Prague, Hamburg, Amsterdam, Italy, and Athens pending. The familiar made unfamiliar -- easier to travel, harder to pay attention. But different cities have different pulses and noticing that is what keeps it interesting.

Stop 01

Paris

Paris -- add photo
The city

Paris is one of those cities where the reputation gets in the way of actually seeing it. It rewards walking without a plan -- the arrondissements away from the main sites have a completely different texture and feel more honest about what the city actually is day to day.

The obvious things
The Eiffel Tower, the Louvre, Notre Dame. Do them -- they earned their status for a reason.
The neighbourhoods
Le Marais, Montmartre, Canal Saint-Martin. The real texture of the city lives here.

Stop 02

Bosnia &
Herzegovina

Mostar bridge -- add photo
Sarajevo

One of the most unexpectedly affecting countries in Europe. Sarajevo wears its recent history in a way that most European cities do not -- the siege, the war, the aftermath are not distant history here. The mix of Ottoman architecture, Austro-Hungarian buildings, and bullet holes in the walls is unlike anywhere else on the continent.

Mostar and its rebuilt bridge are worth the trip alone. The old town is one of the better-preserved Ottoman-era towns in the Balkans.

Sarajevo
The history is recent and visible. The Siege, the war tunnel, the scars on the buildings. One of the most interesting cities in Europe.
Mostar
The old bridge, the old town, the Neretva River. A beautiful and complicated place.

Stop 03

Prague

Old Town -- add photo
Charles Bridge

Prague is one of the most architecturally intact cities in central Europe -- it survived the 20th century largely without being rebuilt, which gives it a coherence that many comparable cities lost. The old town is genuinely beautiful rather than just historically significant.

Old Town Square
The astronomical clock, the Gothic churches, the medieval street plan. Go early before it fills up.
Charles Bridge
At dawn or dusk. In the middle of the day it is hard to move.
The Vltava
Walking along the river gives you a different scale for the city.

Stop 04

Hamburg

Hamburg harbour -- add photo
The city

Hamburg is a port city with the energy that port cities tend to have -- outward-looking, commercially serious, comfortable with being a bit rough at the edges. The Elbphilharmonie is extraordinary. The Reeperbahn is the Reeperbahn. The harbour district gives the city its particular character.

Elbphilharmonie
The concert hall on the harbour. The building itself is as impressive as anything performed inside it.
Speicherstadt
The old warehouse district, now galleries and design studios. Brick canals, good coffee.
The harbour
One of the biggest ports in Europe. The Sunday fish market at dawn is worth the early alarm.

Stop 05

Amsterdam

The canals -- add photo
By bike

A city that works better by bike than anywhere else in Europe. The canal rings, the narrow townhouses, the light on the water in the afternoon. Amsterdam is a well-designed city in the most literal sense -- the structure of it was thought through carefully and it shows.

The canals
Walk or cycle. The Jordaan neighbourhood is the best version of what Amsterdam does.
Rijksmuseum
Rembrandt, Vermeer, the Dutch Golden Age. Worth half a day.
By bike
The only way to move around. Rent one immediately.

Stop 06

Italy

Italy -- add photo
The food

Italy is almost unfair in terms of what it offers per square kilometre. The food, the architecture, the art, the landscape. Every city is different and every city has centuries of things worth paying attention to. It is also a country where slowing down makes more sense than trying to cover ground.

Rome
Two thousand years of history layered on top of each other. The Forum, the Colosseum, the Vatican. Overwhelming in the best way.
The food
Every region is different. The pasta in Bologna, the pizza in Naples, the gelato everywhere. Pay attention to where you are eating.

Stop 07

Athens

Athens -- add photo
The Acropolis

Athens for the Athens Classic Marathon -- a pending chapter. Running the original marathon route to the Panathenaic Stadium is one of those things that earns its place on a list. The city itself, the Acropolis, the neighbourhood below it, the food market. More to come on this one.

Athens Classic Marathon
Running the original route from Marathon to Athens. Pending -- November 2025.
The Acropolis
The Parthenon above the city. More interesting than it sounds even knowing everything about it.
Monastiraki & Plaka
The neighbourhood below the Acropolis. Food, markets, the old city at ground level.
