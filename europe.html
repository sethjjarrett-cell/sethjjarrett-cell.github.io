Region 06

Modern metropolitan Europe

Paris, Bosnia, Hamburg, Amsterdam, Prague, Italy, Greece, Portugal. Weekend breaks and short trips over several years rather than one continuous journey. Europe is easy to take for granted -- Bosnia was the reminder not to.

Stop 01

Paris

Been a few times. I genuinely like Paris -- it has a grandeur that feels earned rather than performed. Le Sacré-Coeur is my favourite spot. That old money, grand European quality is just very much there.

Paris streets
The Eiffel Tower

Been back a few times and it holds up. The monuments are grand, the streets around them even better. Sacré-Coeur and Montmartre have a quality that the more famous landmarks sometimes lack -- a bit quieter, a better view, less of a queue.

Sacré-Coeur and Montmartre
My favourite part of the city. A bit above the tourist centre, better views, a different pace.
The grand stuff
Eiffel Tower, the Louvre, the boulevards. It all earns its reputation.

Stop 02

Bosnia & Herzegovina

The most affecting place I have been to in Europe. Mostar is beautiful -- picturesque bridges, waterways, that Ottoman-meets-European quality. But it still has bullet holes in the walls from a war that ended thirty years ago. Sarajevo even more so.

Mostar bridge
Sarajevo

A friend showed me the exact spot in Sarajevo where Gavrilo Princip stood when he shot Franz Ferdinand -- the footsteps are marked in the pavement. Standing there was one of those moments where history stops being abstract. One decision, one spot, and the twentieth century unfolded the way it did. The contrast throughout Bosnia is stark: somewhere beautiful that was also the site of something horrific within living memory, and has not tried to hide it.

Sarajevo
The war is recent and visible -- bullet holes in buildings everywhere. Stood on the spot where Gavrilo Princip shot Franz Ferdinand. One of those genuinely pivotal moments in history, marked in pavement.
Mostar
Picturesque bridges, waterways, Ottoman streets. Stunning. Still has bullet holes in the walls.

Stop 03

Prague

Birthday trip. Mostly a drinking session, honestly -- lots of beer, an Irish bar we ended up in for most of the night. But the city itself was genuinely nice: old, quaint, pretty. Worth seeing properly at some point.

Old Town Prague
Charles Bridge

We were there to drink and largely succeeded. But between sessions the city itself was hard to ignore -- old streets, Gothic architecture, a scale that feels human rather than overwhelming. I would go back and actually look at it properly.

The old town
Gothic streets, old squares, the astronomical clock. Good backdrop for a birthday weekend.
The beer
Cheap and very good. The main event of the trip, if I am honest.

Stop 04

Hamburg

Just a German experience, really. Good beer, tourist attractions, museums. Felt solidly, unremarkably German in a way that was fine.

Elbphilharmonie
Hamburg harbour

Did the museum circuit, saw the main sights, drank good beer. Hamburg is a proper city -- the harbour is impressive, the Speicherstadt warehouse district has character. It just did not grab me particularly. Pleasant rather than memorable.

The harbour and Speicherstadt
Old warehouse district and a big working port. The most characterful part of the city.
The museums
Did a few. Worth doing to fill a day.
The beer
Good and cheap. The most memorable part of Hamburg.

Stop 05

Amsterdam

Beautiful. The waterways, the bridges, the narrow houses leaning slightly forward over the canals -- it is one of the most picturesque cities in Europe and it knows it. Spent time wandering around and it was genuinely pleasant.

Amsterdam canals
By bike

The canals and bridges are the whole thing -- the city is built around water in a way that makes it feel considered and pretty. Wandered through the Jordaan, along the main canal rings. Just a very pleasant place to be.

The canals and bridges
The whole point of Amsterdam. Walked them for most of the trip. Genuinely picturesque.
The Jordaan
The neighbourhood west of the main canals. The best part of the city to just walk around.

Stop 06

Italy

Venice and then one of the southern islands -- Sicily or thereabouts. Venice is like nothing else: canals instead of roads, the whole thing slightly improbable. The southern island was beaches and heat and very good food.

Rome
Italian food

Venice was as strange and impressive as advertised -- the absence of cars, the canals, the architecture. Got slightly lost, which was fine. The south was a different Italy entirely: slower, hotter, better food, less tourists. Both were good for completely different reasons.

Venice
Canals instead of roads. Slightly improbable as a city. Got lost. That was part of it.
Southern Italy
Slower, hotter, better food, fewer tourists. A different Italy from the north.

Stop 07

Athens

Greece was a beach holiday on Kos -- islands, sun, nothing particularly demanding. The Athens Classic Marathon is coming up in November: running the original route from Marathon to the Panathenaic Stadium in Athens.

The Acropolis
Athens streets

Kos was straightforward -- good beaches, warm water, relaxed. Athens is still to come properly. The marathon is the reason to go, but the Acropolis and the old city will be there alongside it.

Kos
Beach holiday. Good beaches, warm water. Uncomplicated.
Athens Classic Marathon
Running the original route from Marathon to the Panathenaic Stadium. November. Still to come.
Athens
Still to do properly. The marathon will be the occasion.

Stop 08

Portugal

The south -- Lagos and Faro. A beach holiday. Warm, easy, good food. Went canoeing along the coast.

Algarve coast
Lagos

The Algarve coast around Lagos and Faro -- dramatic cliffs, good beaches, canoeing along the rock formations. Warm and straightforward. One of those trips where the point was just to be somewhere sunny and it delivered.

Lagos and Faro
Southern Algarve. Good beaches, good food, warm. Easy trip.
Canoeing the coast
Along the rock arches and sea caves. The best way to see the coastline.
