Region 04

Sleek and ancient Japan

Tokyo, Mount Fuji, Osaka, Kyoto. A country that feels like two entirely different places depending on where you are standing -- and one that rewards paying attention.

TokyoMount FujiOsakaKyoto
Tokyo Mount Fuji Osaka Kyoto

Stop 01

Tokyo

Moves faster than anywhere else on earth. The Shibuya crossing in rush hour is one of those things that only makes sense when you are standing in the middle of it.

Shibuya crossing
Akihabara
7-11 life

Living out of 7/11 is not a hardship here -- Japanese convenience stores are genuinely excellent and part of the culture. Onigiri at 3am is one of the finer experiences the country offers.

The city is enormous and every neighbourhood feels like a different city. Shinjuku, Harajuku, Asakusa, Shimokitazawa -- each one worth a full day.

Shibuya crossing
The famous crossing in rush hour. One of those things that only makes sense when you are in it.
Akihabara
The anime and electronics district. A completely self-contained world.
Living out of 7/11
Japanese convenience stores are exceptional. Part of the experience, not a fallback.
The neighbourhoods
Shinjuku, Harajuku, Asakusa, Shimokitazawa. Each one a different city within the city.

Stop 02

Mount Fuji

A day trip from Tokyo. Fuji from the Five Lakes is one of those views that looks exactly like the pictures and still somehow manages to be more impressive in person.

Fuji from the lake
Day trip

The scale only makes sense when you are standing next to it. The reflection in the lake on a clear morning is the image you have seen a thousand times, and it still does the job.

Fuji Five Lakes
The classic view. Reflections in the lake, the mountain behind. Earns every photograph.
Day trip from Tokyo
Easy by train. Worth the early start.

Stop 03

Osaka

Louder, warmer, more chaotic, better food than Tokyo. The people in Osaka will tell you this themselves and they are not wrong.

Dotonbori
Street food

Dotonbori at night is one of the more overwhelming places in Japan in the best possible way. The food is the whole point -- takoyaki, okonomiyaki, ramen, all done better here than anywhere else.

Dotonbori
Neon, street food, crowds. The centre of Osaka's particular energy.
The food
Takoyaki, okonomiyaki, ramen. Osaka takes its food more seriously than anywhere else in Japan.
Osaka Castle
The castle and park. Worth an afternoon before the food takes over.

Stop 04

Kyoto

Feels like it belongs to a different century. Where Tokyo is relentlessly forward-looking, Kyoto is anchored -- temples, gardens, wooden districts that have been standing for hundreds of years.

Fushimi Inari
Bamboo grove
Kyoto streets

The Fushimi Inari shrine at dawn before the crowds arrive is one of the better decisions you can make in Japan. Thousands of torii gates up a wooded mountain with almost nobody else there.

Fushimi Inari
Thousands of torii gates up the mountain. Go at dawn -- the difference is significant.
Arashiyama bamboo grove
The bamboo forest. Small, worth it, see it early.
Gion
The geisha district. Old Kyoto preserved. Walk it in the evening.
The temples
Kinkaku-ji, Ryoan-ji, Nijo Castle. Pick a few and spend real time.
