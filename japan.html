Region 04

Sleek and ancient Japan

Tokyo, Mount Fuji, Osaka, Kyoto. One of my favourite countries -- alongside Colombia, for completely opposite reasons. Clean, organised, considered. The collective mindset comes through in everything, including the city itself.

TokyoMount FujiOsakaKyoto

Stop 01

Tokyo

One of my favourite cities. The thing that struck me most was how considered it felt -- things placed carefully, space used deliberately, the collective mindset visible in the design of the city itself. Running low on money so I lived largely out of 7-Eleven, which turned out not to be a hardship at all.

Shibuya crossing
Akihabara
7-11 life

The Shibuya crossing is as good as it looks -- pedestrians going in every direction at once, somehow without chaos. Did Akihabara, the anime district, which is a world entirely its own. Went through the different neighbourhoods, each one feeling like a different city. Tokyo is enormous and every part of it is clean and functional in a way that actually makes it pleasant to move around in.

Was running low on money so lived largely out of 7-Eleven. Turned out not to be a hardship -- Japanese convenience stores are genuinely good. Onigiri at odd hours, cheap and excellent. The journey through the mountains on the way to Fuji felt like something out of a Ghibli film.

Shibuya crossing
Pedestrians crossing in every direction at once, no chaos. One of those things that only makes sense when you are standing in it.
Akihabara
The anime and electronics district. A completely self-contained world -- spent a few hours in it.
Living out of 7/11
Was running low on money and lived largely out of these. Turned out not to be a problem -- genuinely excellent food at all hours.
The neighbourhoods
Shinjuku, Harajuku, Asakusa. Each felt like a different city within the city.

Stop 02

Mount Fuji

A day trip from Tokyo. Took the train through the mountains, which felt like something out of a Ghibli film -- cosy, picturesque, that kind of light. Fuji cleared just in time. Walked around the lake and the mountain was just there.

Fuji from the lake
Day trip

The cloud cover had been heavy and cleared just as we arrived at the lake. Walked along the shore with the mountain in full view. It looks exactly like the photographs and still manages to be more than that in person.

Fuji Five Lakes
Walked around the lake with the mountain behind it. Cleared just in time. Looks exactly like every photograph and still surprises you.
The train journey through the mountains
The journey out from Tokyo through the mountains. Cosy, picturesque. Felt like a Ghibli film.

Stop 03

Osaka

Felt a bit stuck in time -- slightly retro, slightly plasticky, like a city that had not quite moved on from the seventies. Still interesting, still worth going. Just did not pull me in the way Tokyo did.

Dotonbori
Street food

Dotonbori at night is neon and street food and crowds. The food was the best part of Osaka. The city itself felt a bit like it had peaked decades ago and not quite updated since.

Dotonbori
Neon, street food, crowds at night. The best part of Osaka.
The food
Takoyaki, okonomiyaki, ramen. The food was genuinely excellent.
Osaka Castle
The castle and park. Spent an afternoon there.

Stop 04

Kyoto

Much better than Osaka. More touristic, but it earns it -- old Japanese streets, temples, the kind of place that actually looks like what you imagine ancient Japan to look like.

Fushimi Inari
Bamboo grove
Kyoto streets

The temples and streets were genuinely impressive -- Fushimi Inari with the torii gates up the mountain, Gion in the evening, the bamboo grove at Arashiyama. Kyoto does what it says it will do, which after Osaka was a relief.

Fushimi Inari
Thousands of torii gates up the mountain. Went early. The difference in crowd numbers was significant.
Arashiyama bamboo grove
The bamboo forest at Arashiyama. Small, but genuinely striking. Went early.
Gion
The geisha district. Old Kyoto preserved -- walked it in the evening.
The temples
Kinkaku-ji, Ryoan-ji, Nijo Castle. Spent proper time at a few rather than rushing through all of them.
