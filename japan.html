Region 04

Sleek and
ancient Japan

Tokyo, Fuji, Osaka, Kyoto. A country that feels like two entirely different places depending on where you are standing -- and one that rewards paying attention more than almost anywhere else.

Stop 01

Tokyo

Shibuya crossing -- add photo
Akihabara anime district
7/11 life

Tokyo moves faster than anywhere else on earth. The Shibuya crossing in rush hour is one of those things that only makes sense when you are standing in the middle of it. The anime district in Akihabara is a world entirely unto itself.

Living out of 7/11 is not a hardship here -- the convenience stores in Japan are genuinely excellent and part of the culture rather than a last resort. The city is enormous and every neighbourhood feels like a different city.

Shibuya crossing
The famous crossing in rush hour. One of those things that only makes sense when you are in it.
Akihabara
The anime and electronics district. A completely self-contained world.
Living out of 7/11
Japanese convenience stores are exceptional. Onigiri, ramen, hot food at 3am. Part of the experience.
The neighbourhoods
Shinjuku, Harajuku, Asakusa, Shimokitazawa. Each one a different city within the city.

Stop 02

Mount Fuji

Fuji from the lake -- add photo
Day trip

A day trip from Tokyo. Fuji from the Fuji Five Lakes is one of those views that looks exactly like the pictures and somehow still manages to be more impressive in person. The scale only makes sense when you are standing next to it.

Fuji Five Lakes
The classic view. Reflections in the lake, the mountain behind. Earns every photograph.
Day trip from Tokyo
Easy enough by train. Worth the early start.

Stop 03

Osaka

Dotonbori -- add photo
Street food

Osaka has a completely different energy from Tokyo. Louder, warmer, more chaotic, better food. The people in Osaka will tell you this themselves and they are not wrong. Dotonbori at night is one of the more overwhelming places in Japan in the best possible way.

Dotonbori
Neon, street food, crowds. The centre of Osaka's particular energy.
The food
Takoyaki, okonomiyaki, ramen. Osaka takes its food more seriously than anywhere else in Japan.

Stop 04

Kyoto

Fushimi Inari -- add photo
Bamboo grove
Gion district

Kyoto feels like it belongs to a different century. Where Tokyo is relentlessly forward-looking, Kyoto is anchored -- temples, gardens, wooden districts that have been standing for hundreds of years. The Fushimi Inari shrine at dawn before the crowds arrive is one of the better decisions you can make in Japan.

Fushimi Inari
Thousands of torii gates up the mountain. Go at dawn -- the difference is significant.
Arashiyama bamboo grove
The bamboo forest. Small, worth it, see it early.
Gion
The geisha district. Old Kyoto preserved. Walk it in the evening.
The temples
Kinkaku-ji, Ryoan-ji, Nijo Castle. There are too many to see everything. Pick a few and spend real time.
