Region 07

South America

Colombia, Peru, Bolivia. Mountains, rainforest, salt flats, ayahuasca -- and the continent where the engineering work stopped being abstract.

ColombiaPeruBolivia
Colombia Peru Bolivia

Stop 01

Colombia

Colombia surprised me more than almost anywhere on the trip. Bogota is a proper metropolis with a cultural life most people in Europe do not know exists.

Medellin
Salento
North Colombia

Medellin went from one of the most dangerous cities in the world to one of the most innovative in South America in twenty years -- the story of how is worth understanding.

Salento and the coffee region are some of the most beautiful landscape on the continent. The Valle de Cocora does not look like a real place.

Bogota
La Candelaria, the Gold Museum, the food scene. A real city. Underrated.
North Colombia
The Caribbean coast, Cartagena, Tayrona. A completely different climate from the interior.
Medellin
The transformation is worth reading about before you arrive. One of the most interesting cities in South America.
Salento
Coffee farms, wax palms, mountains. The Valle de Cocora does not look like a real place.

Stop 02

Peru

One of the most varied countries on the continent. Lima has a serious food scene that most people do not expect.

Huacachina oasis
Sacred Valley
Cusco

Huacachina is a genuine oasis in the middle of sand dunes -- sandboarding at sunset is one of those things that sounds ridiculous and turns out to be excellent.

Cusco and the Sacred Valley deserve more time than most people give them. The dog shelter in the Sacred Valley was an unexpected afternoon that turned into something more.

Lima
One of the best food cities in South America. Ceviche, pisco sours, Miraflores.
Huacachina
A natural oasis surrounded by giant sand dunes. Sandboarding at sunset. One of the stranger and better afternoons of the trip.
Cusco
The old Inca capital. The altitude hits immediately -- give yourself a day.
Sacred Valley & dog shelter
The valley between Cusco and Machu Picchu, and an unplanned afternoon at a dog rescue that became one of the better stops of the trip.

Stop 03

Bolivia

The hardest and most rewarding country in South America. La Paz sits at over 3,600 metres and the altitude is not a joke.

Salt flats
Huayna Potosi
La Paz

Huayna Potosi is a 6,088 metre peak outside La Paz -- one of the more accessible high-altitude climbs in the world, though that is relative.

The salt flats at Salar de Uyuni are one of the great landscapes on earth -- completely flat, completely white, and enormous beyond what a photograph can convey.

La Paz
The world's highest capital. Built into a canyon. Cable cars, witches market, the Death Road.
Huayna Potosi
6,088 metres. Two days, crampons, summit at 3am. Physically the hardest thing on the trip.
The Amazonas
The jungle. Nothing else to say -- you have to go in.
Ayahuasca
A ceremony in the jungle. Treat it with seriousness. A significant experience.
Sucre
Bolivia's constitutional capital. White colonial city, much warmer than La Paz.
Mount Potosi
The silver mountain that funded the Spanish empire for three centuries. The mines are still operating.
Salar de Uyuni
The salt flats. White to every horizon. One of the great landscapes on earth.
