Region 07

South America

Colombia, Peru, Bolivia. Mountains, rainforest, salt flats, ayahuasca, and the continent where the engineering work stopped being abstract. Communities without reliable power are not a case study when you have spent time in them.

Country 01

Colombia

Medellin -- add photo
Salento coffee region
North Colombia

Colombia surprised me more than almost anywhere on the trip. Bogota is a proper metropolis with a cultural life that most people in Europe do not know exists. Medellin went from one of the most dangerous cities in the world to one of the most innovative in South America in the space of twenty years -- the story of how is worth understanding.

Salento and the coffee region in the Eje Cafetero is some of the most beautiful landscape on the continent. The Valle de Cocora -- a valley of giant wax palms -- is one of those places that does not look real.

Bogota
A real city with real culture. La Candelaria, the Gold Museum, the food scene. Underrated.
North Colombia
The Caribbean coast, Cartagena, Tayrona. A completely different climate and pace from the interior.
Medellin
The transformation is worth reading about before you arrive. The city is extraordinary for what it has done with a recent history that could have gone differently.
Salento
Coffee farms, wax palms, mountains. The Valle de Cocora does not look like a real place.

Country 02

Peru

Huacachina oasis -- add photo
Sacred Valley
Machu Picchu / Cusco

Peru is one of the most varied countries on the continent. Lima has a serious food scene that most people do not expect. Huacachina is a genuine oasis in the middle of sand dunes -- sandboarding down them at sunset is one of those things that sounds ridiculous and turns out to be excellent.

Cusco and the Sacred Valley deserve more time than most people give them. Machu Picchu earns its place as one of the great sites in the world. And the dog shelter in the Sacred Valley was an unexpected afternoon that turned into something more.

Lima
One of the best food cities in South America. Ceviche, pisco sours, Miraflores. Do not skip it.
Huacachina
A natural oasis surrounded by giant sand dunes. Sandboarding at sunset. One of the stranger and better afternoons of the trip.
Cusco
The old Inca capital, now a colonial city built on Inca foundations. The altitude hits immediately -- give yourself a day.
The Sacred Valley & dog shelter
The valley between Cusco and Machu Picchu. We ended up at a dog rescue shelter for an afternoon -- one of the better unplanned stops of the whole trip.

Country 03

Bolivia

Salt flats -- add photo
Huayna Potosi
The Amazon

Bolivia is the hardest and most rewarding country in South America. La Paz sits at over 3,600 metres and the altitude is not a joke -- the city is extraordinary in its setting, built into a canyon, the cable cars linking the wealthier districts on the rim to the city below.

Huayna Potosi is a 6,088 metre peak outside La Paz -- one of the more accessible high-altitude climbs in the world, though that is relative. The Amazonas was something else entirely. And then ayahuasca, which is its own chapter. The salt flats at Salar de Uyuni are one of the great landscapes on earth -- completely flat, completely white, and enormous beyond what a photograph can convey.

La Paz
The world's highest capital city. Built into a canyon. Cable cars, witches market, the Death Road. One of the most unusual cities in South America.
Huayna Potosi
6,088 metres. Two days of climbing, crampons, the summit at 3am. Physically the hardest thing on the trip.
The Amazonas
The jungle. Nothing else to say about it -- you have to go in.
Ayahuasca
A ceremony in the jungle. Not a tourist activity -- treat it with the seriousness it deserves. A significant experience.
Sucre
Bolivia's constitutional capital, white colonial city, much warmer and calmer than La Paz.
Mount Potosi
The silver mountain that funded the Spanish empire for three centuries. The mines are still operating and the history is brutal and worth knowing.
Salar de Uyuni
The salt flats. The largest in the world. White to every horizon. One of the great landscapes on earth and impossible to photograph in a way that does it justice.
