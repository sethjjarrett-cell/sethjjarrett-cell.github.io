Region 02

South & Southeast Asia

Nepal, Cambodia, Thailand, Laos, Vietnam, Malaysia, Singapore, Philippines, Indonesia. The region that probably changes you most the first time.

NepalCambodiaThailand LaosVietnamMalaysia SingaporePhilippinesIndonesia
Nepal Cambodia Thailand Laos Vietnam Malaysia Singapore Philippines Indonesia

Stop 01

Nepal

Kathmandu is chaotic in the best way. Then Pokhara, and the Annapurna circuit beyond it. The mountains at that scale do something to your sense of proportion.

Kathmandu
Annapurna trek

The circuit is not the hardest trek in Nepal but it is one of the most spectacular. The landscape shifts constantly.

Kathmandu
Temples, dust, colour and chaos. A city that has been building for a thousand years.
Pokhara
Gateway to the Himalayas. Calmer than Kathmandu, spectacular differently.
Annapurna
The circuit. Mountains at a scale that makes everything else feel manageable.

Stop 02

Cambodia

A country that wears its history visibly. Strange abandoned places in the jungle make more sense once you understand what happened there.

Angkor Wat
Koh Rong
Kep

Then the islands, which are a completely different story. Koh Rong is white sand, clear water and nights that go on too long.

Phnom Penh
The capital. The history is heavy and worth sitting with.
Kep
Southern coast, natural park, Khmer Rouge-era buildings half-swallowed by jungle.
Koh Rong
Islands off the coast. Sunny, remote, hard to leave.
Siem Reap
Angkor Wat. Get up before sunrise.

Stop 03

Thailand

Thailand in both directions. The north is temples, mountains and Pai. The south is Christmas on Koh Tao, new year at the full moon party, Muay Thai, and boxing a friend at a bar on Phi Phi that turned out to have a ring.

Muay Thai
Full moon party
Phi Phi

Finding the beach from The Beach on a long-tail at dawn was one of those things that earns its place in the story.

Bangkok
Enormous, chaotic, endlessly interesting.
Chiang Mai and Pai
Northern Thailand. Temples, mountains, a town worth staying in too long.
Koh Tao
Christmas here. Diving and quiet islands.
Full Moon Party
New Year. Once is enough and worth it.
Muay Thai camp
Trained for a week. One of the better decisions.
Phi Phi and The Beach
Boxing match at a reggae bar then Maya Bay at dawn.

Stop 04

Laos

The quietest country in the region and one of the most beautiful. Luang Prabang has a particular stillness.

Luang Prabang
Nong Khiaw

Nong Khiaw sits between karst mountains and feels completely removed from the world. One of the most beautiful places on the trip.

Luang Prabang
Temples, the Mekong, monks at sunrise. Rewards moving slowly.
Nong Khiaw
Karst limestone mountains, river, almost no one. One of the best stops.
Vang Vieng
Tubing, bars, backpackers. Part of the story.

Stop 05

Vietnam

The Ha Giang Loop is a highlight of the whole trip -- mountain roads in the far north that almost nobody is on. Rice terraces and landscapes that look like paintings.

Ha Giang Loop
Ha Long Bay
Hai Van Pass

Ha Long Bay lives up to it, Hue has the best food, and the Hai Van Pass is the kind of road that makes you understand why people love motorbikes.

Hanoi
The north. Busy, layered, excellent food.
Ha Giang Loop
Far north on a motorbike. Arguably the best thing on the whole trip.
Ha Long Bay
Limestone karsts from the water. Earned its reputation.
Hue, Hai Van Pass and Da Nang
The pass is the ride. Hue has the best food in Vietnam.
Ho Chi Minh City
The south. Faster, louder. The war museums are uncomfortable -- which is the point.

Stop 06

Malaysia

Three very different places. Langkawi for islands and quiet. Georgetown for the food, street art, and one of the most interesting cities in the region.

Georgetown street art
Langkawi

Kuala Lumpur as a proper metropolis that often gets skipped over on the way somewhere else.

Langkawi
Islands, mangroves, duty-free. A good place to slow down.
Georgetown, Penang
The food capital of Southeast Asia. The street art is worth the trip alone.
Kuala Lumpur
A proper modern city. Worth more than a night.

Stop 07

Singapore

The cleanest city in Asia. Both part of Southeast Asia and completely separate from it.

Singapore skyline
Gardens by the Bay

Worth a few days to understand how it works and why it feels the way it does.

Singapore
Spotless, expensive, fascinating. Engineered into something singular.
Gardens by the Bay
The supertrees at night. One of the more surreal things you can walk through.
The hawker centres
Cheap, excellent food everywhere. One of the great institutions of the city.

Stop 08

Philippines

Manila is chaotic and worth spending time in. Palawan is the reason most people come.

Palawan
El Nido

Limestone cliffs, clear water, island hopping between places that look like they were designed to be photographed.

Manila
Chaotic, layered, often skipped. Worth a day or two of attention.
Palawan
El Nido, island hopping, lagoons. Some of the best water in the region.

Stop 09

Indonesia

Bali is more than the Instagram version. The temples, rice terraces, and culture underneath the tourism are worth finding.

Bali
Gili T

Gili T is a small island with no motorised vehicles, excellent diving, and the kind of pace that makes everything slow down.

Bali
More than the cliches. The cultural layer underneath is genuinely interesting.
Gili Trawangan
No cars, no motorbikes, good diving, great sunsets.
