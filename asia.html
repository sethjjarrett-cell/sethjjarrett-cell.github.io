Region 02

South & Southeast
Asia

Nepal, Cambodia, Thailand, Laos, Vietnam, Malaysia, Singapore, Philippines, Indonesia. The region that probably changes you most the first time -- the density of life, the heat, the contrast between the tourist trail and everything just off it.

Stop 01

Nepal

Kathmandu -- add photo
Annapurna trek

Kathmandu is chaotic in the best way -- a city that layers centuries on top of each other without apology. Then Pokhara, and the Annapurna circuit beyond it. The mountains at that scale do something to your sense of proportion.

Kathmandu
Temples, dust, colour and chaos. A city that has been building for a thousand years and shows it.
Pokhara
Gateway to the Himalayas. Calmer than Kathmandu, spectacular in a completely different way.
Annapurna
The circuit. Mountains at a scale that makes everything else feel manageable by comparison.

Stop 02

Cambodia

Angkor Wat -- add photo
Koh Rong islands
Kep

A country that wears its history visibly -- the war left marks that you see in Phnom Penh and feel in the south around Kep. Strange, abandoned places in the jungle that make more sense once you understand what happened there.

Then the islands, which are a completely different story. Koh Rong is all white sand, clear water and nights that go on too long.

Phnom Penh
The capital. The history is heavy and worth sitting with rather than moving through quickly.
Kep
Southern coast, natural park, abandoned buildings from the Khmer Rouge era half-swallowed by jungle.
Koh Rong
Islands off the coast. Sunny, remote, and the kind of place that makes it hard to leave.
Siem Reap
Angkor Wat. Gets up before sunrise and go -- it earns the reputation.

Stop 03

Thailand

Muay Thai camp -- add photo
Full moon party
Phi Phi / The Beach

Thailand in both directions. The north is temples, mountains and Pai, which is one of those places that people end up staying in far longer than planned. Chiang Mai is a proper city with depth beyond the tourist circuit.

The south is a different world. Christmas on Koh Tao, new year at the full moon party, a Muay Thai camp, boxing a friend at a bar on Phi Phi that turned out to be a boxing ring, and finding the beach from The Beach.

Bangkok
Enormous, chaotic, endlessly interesting. Worth more time than most people give it.
Chiang Mai & Pai
Northern Thailand at its best. Temples in the city, mountains beyond it, Pai for slowing down.
Koh Tao
Christmas here. Diving, calm water, the kind of quiet that the islands do well.
Full Moon Party
New Year. Koh Phangan at its most unhinged. Worth doing once.
Muay Thai camp
Trained for a week. One of the better decisions of the trip.
Phuket, Phi Phi & The Beach
Phi Phi included a boxing match against a friend at a reggae bar that turned out to have a ring. Found Maya Bay -- the beach from the film -- on a long-tail at dawn.

Stop 04

Laos

Luang Prabang -- add photo
Nong Khiaw karst mountains

The quietest country in the region and one of the most beautiful. Luang Prabang has a particular stillness, especially early morning when the monks walk for alms. Nong Khiaw sits between karst mountains and feels completely removed from the world. Vang Vieng is the party town -- a different kind of experience entirely.

Luang Prabang
Temples, the Mekong, monks at sunrise. A town that rewards moving slowly.
Nong Khiaw
Karst limestone mountains, river, almost no one. One of the most beautiful places on the trip.
Vang Vieng
Tubing, bars, backpackers. A completely different energy -- but part of the story.

Stop 05

Vietnam

Ha Giang Loop -- add photo
Ha Long Bay
Hai Van Pass

Vietnam on a motorbike from north to south is one of the better ways to see a country. The Ha Giang Loop is a highlight of the whole trip -- mountain roads in the far north that almost nobody is on, through villages and rice terraces and landscapes that look like paintings.

Ha Long Bay lives up to it, Hue has the best food, and the Hai Van Pass between Hue and Da Nang is the kind of road that makes you understand why people love motorbikes.

Hanoi
The north. Busy, layered, excellent food. A proper city rather than just a starting point.
Ha Giang Loop
Far north Vietnam on a motorbike. Arguably the best thing on the whole trip -- mountain roads, rice terraces, almost no tourists.
Ha Long Bay
Limestone karsts rising from the water. Earned its reputation.
Hue, Hai Van Pass & Da Nang
The pass is the ride. Hue has the best food in Vietnam, which is saying something.
Ho Chi Minh City
The south. Faster, louder, more modern. The war museums are not comfortable -- which is the point.

Stop 06

Malaysia

Georgetown street art -- add photo
Langkawi

Three very different places. Langkawi for the islands and the quiet. Georgetown on Penang for the food, the street art, and one of the most interesting cities in the region. Kuala Lumpur as a proper metropolis that often gets skipped over on the way somewhere else.

Langkawi
Islands, mangroves, duty-free. A good place to slow down after Vietnam.
Georgetown, Penang
The food capital of Southeast Asia. The street art is worth the trip alone.
Kuala Lumpur
A proper modern city that most people fly through. Worth more than a night.

Stop 07

Singapore

Singapore -- add photo
Gardens by the Bay

The cleanest city in Asia and one of the most efficiently run places on earth. The contrast with everywhere else in the region is stark -- it is both part of Southeast Asia and completely separate from it. Worth a few days to understand how it works and why it feels the way it does.

Singapore
Spotless, expensive, fascinating. A city-state that has engineered itself into something singular.

Stop 08

Philippines

Palawan -- add photo
El Nido

Manila is chaotic and worth spending time in rather than rushing through on the way to the islands. Palawan is the reason most people come -- limestone cliffs, clear water, island hopping between places that look like they were designed to be photographed.

Manila
Chaotic, layered, often skipped. Worth a day or two of actual attention.
Palawan
El Nido, island hopping, lagoons. Some of the best water in the region.

Stop 09

Indonesia

Bali -- add photo
Gili T

Bali is more than it gets credit for beyond the Instagram version of it. The temples, the rice terraces, the culture underneath the tourism are worth finding. Gili T is a small island with no motorised vehicles, excellent diving, and the kind of pace that makes everything slow down.

Bali
More than the cliches. The cultural layer underneath the tourist surface is genuinely interesting.
Gili Trawangan
No cars, no motorbikes, good diving, great sunsets. A proper island escape.
