Region 05

Africa -- Morocco

Casablanca and Marrakech. White walls, heat, the medina, the souks. Morocco sits at the edge of two worlds and makes no attempt to resolve the tension between them.

Stop 01

Casablanca

Sleek and white in a way that feels deliberate. Morocco's modern face -- the financial capital, the biggest city, the one that looks outward.

Hassan II Mosque
The white city

The Hassan II Mosque is one of the largest in the world and sits over the Atlantic in a way that stops you. It is worth spending time in Casablanca to understand the country before heading inland.

Hassan II Mosque
Built over the Atlantic. One of the most impressive buildings in Africa.
The Corniche
The seafront promenade. A different side of Morocco from the medinas.
The old medina
Smaller than Marrakech, less chaotic, easier to navigate. A good introduction.

Stop 02

Marrakech

Overwhelming in the way that only a few cities manage. The medina is a labyrinth that takes days to start making sense of.

Jemaa el-Fna
The souks
Medina

Jemaa el-Fna at night is one of the great squares in the world -- storytellers, snake charmers, food stalls, and the particular energy of a place that has been doing this for centuries.

The heat makes everything more intense. The colours -- terracotta, saffron, deep blue -- are exactly as vivid as they look in photographs and somehow still surprising in person.

Jemaa el-Fna
The main square at night. One of the great public spaces in the world.
The souks
Leather, spices, ceramics, textiles. Get lost in them. That is the point.
The medina
Old city, riads, narrow lanes, unexpected courtyards.
Majorelle Garden
Cobalt blue, cacti. A quiet corner in a loud city.
