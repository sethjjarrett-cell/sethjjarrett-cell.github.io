Region 05

Africa --
Morocco

Casablanca and Marrakech. White walls, heat, the medina, the souks. Morocco sits at the edge of two worlds and makes no attempt to resolve the tension between them.

Stop 01

Casablanca

Hassan II Mosque -- add photo
The white city

Sleek and white in a way that feels deliberate. Casablanca is Morocco's modern face -- the financial capital, the biggest city, the one that looks outward. The Hassan II Mosque is one of the largest in the world and sits over the Atlantic in a way that stops you.

It is not the Morocco most people come for but it is worth spending time in to understand the country before heading inland.

Hassan II Mosque
Built over the Atlantic. One of the most impressive buildings in Africa -- the scale and the setting are extraordinary.
The Corniche
The seafront promenade. A different side of Morocco from the medinas.
The old medina
Smaller than Marrakech, less chaotic, easier to navigate. A good introduction.

Stop 02

Marrakech

Jemaa el-Fna -- add photo
The souks
Medina

Marrakech is overwhelming in the way that only a few cities in the world manage. The medina is a labyrinth that takes days to start making sense of. Jemaa el-Fna at night is one of the great squares in the world -- storytellers, snake charmers, food stalls, and the particular energy of a place that has been doing this for centuries.

The heat makes everything more intense. The colours -- terracotta, saffron, deep blue -- are exactly as vivid as they look in photographs and somehow still surprising in person.

Jemaa el-Fna
The main square at night. One of the great public spaces in the world -- go after dark when it really starts.
The souks
Leather, spices, ceramics, textiles. Get lost in them. That is the point.
The medina
The old city. Riads, narrow lanes, unexpected courtyards. Days of wandering.
The Majorelle Garden
Cobalt blue, cacti, YSL's garden. A quiet corner in a loud city.
