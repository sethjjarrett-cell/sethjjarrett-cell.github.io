Region 03

Australia

Four months. Most of it in Melbourne, working in a multi-level bar and working out how a city works when you have no one in it. Then the Great Ocean Road south, Sydney, and up to Cairns and Port Douglas for the Great Barrier Reef.

Stop 01

Melbourne

Spent most of the four months here. Getting a job was harder than expected -- handed CVs into bar after bar, eventually emailed one on a whim because I could not face walking in. They gave me a trial shift and I got it. Work was inconsistent but Melbourne itself was very good.

Melbourne rewards actually living in it rather than passing through -- the coffee is cheap and excellent, the food is a proper melting pot, and the nightlife has real range. There is a bar that runs twenty-four hours on weekends, which is an experience. The laneways are the best part of the city: street art, hidden bars, the kind of place that takes a few weeks to find.

Working in hospitality
Multi-level venue in the city centre. Hard to get, inconsistent hours, but a good crash course in working under pressure with people you have never met.
The laneways
Street art, coffee, hidden bars. Takes a few weeks to find but worth finding.
The culture
AFL, food markets, art galleries. A city that takes its culture seriously in the best way.

Stop 02

The East Coast

Drove the Great Ocean Road south of Melbourne -- cliffs, dramatic coast, the Twelve Apostles. One of the better drives I have done. Then later up to Sydney, which is a different thing entirely.

The Great Ocean Road runs southwest along the coast from Melbourne. Cliffs dropping into the Southern Ocean, the Twelve Apostles stacks, small towns. Genuinely one of the better drives I have done anywhere.

The Great Ocean Road
Cliffs, the Southern Ocean, the Twelve Apostles. One of the better drives I have done.
East coast road trip
Drove south along the coast from Melbourne. Genuinely dramatic landscape -- takes you by surprise.

Stop 03

Sydney

Went up to visit a friend. Aesthetically one of the nicest cities I have been to -- the harbour is genuinely beautiful. Felt a bit like it knew that and traded on it. The people felt slightly more closed off than Melbourne. Good for a few days.

More obviously beautiful than Melbourne, less texture. The harbour earns every photograph. Went to a party out near the Blue Mountains, which was good -- colder than expected out there. Made the mistake of looking for spiders under a house, which was not a good idea.

Sydney Harbour
The Bridge, the Opera House, the water. It all looks exactly as advertised.
Bondi
The beach everyone talks about. Went. It is what it is.
The ferry network
Took the ferries across the harbour. Good way to see it.

Stop 04

Cairns & Port Douglas

Flew up to do the Great Barrier Reef. Cairns itself is not particularly nice -- it has the feel of a place that exists mainly as a staging post for people doing their regional visa hours. Port Douglas up the road was a different story: genuinely beautiful beaches and a quieter pace.

Went scuba diving on the Great Barrier Reef. Bucket list item, so it needed doing -- I found it a bit overrated in honesty, but the scale of it is undeniable. Port Douglas had stunning beaches and felt more like somewhere to actually be rather than pass through. Cairns had a particular loneliness to it: full of people grinding regional visa hours in rural areas, all a bit transient. Not uninteresting, but not somewhere I would go back to specifically.

Great Barrier Reef
Scuba diving. Bucket list, needed doing. A bit overrated, but the scale is real.
Port Douglas
Stunning beaches up the coast from Cairns. Much nicer than Cairns itself.
Cairns
Gateway to the reef. Functional rather than beautiful. Full of people doing their regional visa hours.
