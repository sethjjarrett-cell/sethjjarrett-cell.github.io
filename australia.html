Australia

Four months living and working in Melbourne, then travelling the east coast. Cool, sunny, and the place that taught me most about integrating quickly into a team where you know nobody.

Stop 01

Melbourne

Melbourne -- add photo
The venue
City life

Spent most of the Australian chapter in Melbourne, working in a multi-level venue in the city. Eight hours on your feet in a busy bar is its own education -- in people, pressure, and the ability to integrate quickly into a team where you know nobody.

Melbourne itself is one of those cities that rewards actually living in it rather than passing through. The coffee culture, the laneways, the sport, the food. It has a texture that takes a few weeks to find.

Working in hospitality
Multi-level venue in the city centre. The best crash course in communication and composure under pressure.
The laneways
Street art, coffee, hidden bars. The part of Melbourne that takes a few weeks to find.
Sport & culture
AFL, cricket, food markets. A city that takes its culture seriously in the best way.

Stop 02

The East Coast

East coast road -- add photo
Beaches

Travelling the east coast after Melbourne -- the classic route but classic for good reason. The landscape shifts constantly and the distances between places give you time to think. Australia is beautiful in a way that takes you by surprise if you have only seen it in photos.

The Great Ocean Road
Cliffs, waves, the Twelve Apostles. One of the better drives in the world.
East coast road trip
North from Melbourne to Sydney. Long stretches of coast, small towns, places that feel genuinely remote.

Stop 03

Sydney

Sydney Harbour -- add photo
Opera House

Sydney is everything the postcard suggests and then some. The harbour, the Opera House, Bondi -- it is a city that knows what it is and makes no apologies for it. Warmer than Melbourne, more obviously beautiful, and a good place to end the Australian chapter.

Sydney Harbour
The Bridge, the Opera House, the water. Earns every photograph taken of it.
Bondi
The beach everyone talks about. Still worth it.
