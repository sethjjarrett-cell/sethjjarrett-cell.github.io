§1.1

Wind–Solar Mini-Grid — Feasibility Study

Individual · 83% · Cohort exemplar

Abstract. An independent, end-to-end feasibility study of a hybrid wind–solar mini-grid, assessed across four load scenarios. The work covers resource modelling, storage optimisation, and full lifetime cost analysis, and was later used as an exemplar paper for the following cohort.

  • Wind resource modelled with Weibull distributions to estimate generation across the candidate site
  • Battery depth-of-discharge and lifecycle optimisation evaluated across all four load scenarios
  • Full cost analysis: capital expenditure, operation & maintenance, and payback period per scenario
Worked example Method in brief — from wind data to payback period ▸

The study works forward from resource to economics. Site wind speeds are fitted to Weibull distributions, which feed expected turbine output; solar generation is sized against the same demand profiles. The storage question is treated as an optimisation: deeper battery discharge means fewer cells but shorter life, so depth-of-discharge is traded against replacement cost across the battery’s cycle curve.

Each of the four load scenarios is then carried through to a full costing — capex, O&M, and payback — so the comparison at the end is economic, not just technical. The result is a recommendation grounded in which configuration actually pays for itself, and when.

§1.2

Solar DC Microgrid — Design & Build

Group industrial project · 40 credits

Abstract. Co-designed and helped build a solar DC microgrid for a remote off-grid community in the Brazilian Amazon — the full lifecycle from concept through simulation to validated hardware.

  • Led PV sizing, battery selection, wire sizing, and the complete system specification
  • MATLAB algorithms sized battery banks and arrays using five years of real irradiance data
  • Simulink model of MPPT, droop control, and a bidirectional BMS — validated against a physical PCB prototype
Worked example Method in brief — from irradiance data to working hardware ▸

Sizing was driven by data rather than rules of thumb: five years of measured irradiance fed MATLAB routines that sized the array and battery bank against the community’s demand profile, including the bad weeks, not just the average ones.

The control system — maximum power point tracking, droop control for load sharing, and a bidirectional battery management system — was modelled in Simulink and then tested for real against a PCB prototype the team built. Closing that loop between simulation and physical hardware was the point of the project: the model had to survive contact with a soldering iron.
