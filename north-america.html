Region 01  /  First stop

North
America

New York, Washington, Miami. A family trip -- mum, sister, brother. First time in America. The scale of it, the disparity of it, and losing a bank card in Washington while someone spent two thousand pounds in Target.

New York Washington DC Miami

Stop 01

New York

First stop, family trip. New York is enormous and grand in a way that photographs do not quite convey. What struck me most was the disparity -- serious wealth and serious poverty side by side, people shooting up on the street in a way you just do not see in London. A different level of it.

The Empire State Building -- one of the biggest buildings in the world. Went up for the view. The city from above changes how you understand it at street level.
Central Park -- enormous. Spent a good stretch of a day in it. The Natural History Museum alongside it was genuinely good.
The disparity -- wealth and homelessness in the same street. People shooting up and smoking crack in a way that does not happen visibly in London. A different scale of it.
Natural History Museum -- very good. One of the better museums I have been to.
Brooklyn Bridge and Brooklyn -- walked across and into the start of Brooklyn. The view back across the water to Manhattan is one of the better city views I have seen.

Stop 02

Washington DC

After New York. Visited Arlington Cemetery, the monuments, the tourist circuit. A very considered, grand city. Also where I lost my bank card -- someone found it and spent about two thousand pounds in Target within the hour. A mistake you only make once.

Arlington Cemetery -- add photo
The Monument
Arlington Cemetery -- rows of identical white headstones as far as you can see. Genuinely moving in a way that is hard to explain until you are standing in it.
The monuments and the Mall -- the Lincoln Memorial, the Washington Monument, the grand civic scale of it. Saw the churches and sights. A city that takes its own importance seriously.
Lost the bank card -- dropped it somewhere and someone ran up about two thousand pounds in Target before I had noticed. Make a mistake and you pay for it.

Stop 03

Miami

Very hot, very multicultural, a particular energy to it -- chains, Spanish music, Cuban influence all over the place. Miami Beach was a bit oversaturated and expensive. Wynwood was much better: cool clothes stores, murals, more of a real neighbourhood.

The clothes
Miami Beach -- very hot, very expensive, slightly oversaturated. The Cuban and Latin influence gives it a specific energy that is unlike anywhere else in America.
Wynwood -- much better than Miami Beach. Cool clothes stores, murals, a neighbourhood with actual character. Glad I went there rather than just staying on the beach strip.
The energy -- chains, Spanish music playing everywhere, Cuban culture running through everything. Baller in a way that feels genuinely specific to that city.
