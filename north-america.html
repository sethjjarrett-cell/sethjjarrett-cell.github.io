Stop 01

New York

First stop. The city that every film, every show, every reference you've ever seen suddenly becomes real — and then immediately feels smaller and bigger at the same time.

📷 Empire State
📷 Central Park

Times Square — exactly what you expect, completely unlike what you expect. The scale of it only makes sense in person.
Empire State Building — worth it purely for the view. The city from above changes how you understand it at street level.
Rally outside Trump Tower — stumbled into a protest on Fifth Avenue. Not something you forget.
Central Park and the Natural History Museum — two things that deserve more time than you give them.
Brooklyn — across the bridge for the view back. The city looks completely different from the other side.

Stop 02

Washington DC

A city built to project power, with a cemetery that puts all of it in context. Also the place my wallet was stolen — a lesson in paying attention.

📷 Arlington Cemetery — add your photo
📷 The Monument
Arlington Cemetery — extensive and genuinely moving. Row after row of identical white headstones does something to you.
The Monument and Mall — the grand civic scale of it. Every capital city has this but Washington does it with particular conviction.
Wallet stolen — the first lesson of solo travel. Replaceable. The story is better than the wallet was.

Stop 03

Miami

Miami Beach, Wynwood, and the particular kind of energy that comes from a city that runs entirely on sun and confidence.

📷 Miami Beach
📷 Wynwood Walls
📷 The clothes
Miami Beach — the art deco strip, the ocean, the heat. A place that takes itself completely seriously.
Wynwood — murals, galleries, good food. The neighbourhood that feels most like a real city under the surface.
The clothes — Miami dresses differently from anywhere else in America. You notice it immediately.
